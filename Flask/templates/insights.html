Sentiment Analysis on President Trump’s tweets and its impact on Stock Price

Objective

Explore the impact on stock prices casued by president Trump's tweets about companies S&P 500

Challenges

  • Limitation of twitter API (free account) to access > 3600 tweets for any tweet handle.
  • The unstructured nature of tweets, their brevity, and the frequent use of abbreviations mean that distinguishing words from certain company names (Intel the company from intel the abbreviation for intelligence).
  • Tweets about subsidiaries, products or other relevant keywords uniquely related to a company.
  • Sentiment Analysis accuracy is limited to the training data set provided.

    • Lockheed Martin

    President Trump decried defense company Lockheed Martin on Twitter for its cost and budget overruns.

    Based on the tremendous cost and cost overruns of the Lockheed Martin F-35, I have asked Boeing to price-out a comparable F-18 Super Hornet!

    — Donald J. Trump (@realDonaldTrump) December 22, 2016
    Boeing

    Trump tweeted criticism about the cost of a new Air Force One buit by Boeing

    Boeing is building a brand new 747 Air Force One for future presidents, but costs are out of control, more than $4 billion. Cancel order!

    — Donald J. Trump (@realDonaldTrump) December 6, 2016

    Toyota

    When Toyota announced it was building a new plant in Mexico, Trump took to Twitter to blast the company.

    Toyota Motor said will build a new plant in Baja, Mexico, to build Corolla cars for U.S. NO WAY! Build plant in U.S. or pay big border tax.

    — Donald J. Trump (@realDonaldTrump) January 5, 2017

    Observations

  • Preliminary exploration of Donald Trump's tweets that refer to S&P 500 companies does not find very strong evidence for the contention that tweets that are critical of a company results in a decline in its stock price or that tweets that praise a company results in a rise.
  • In all cases, regardless of whether Trump’s tweets were positive or negative, the changes in company stock prices were nominal.
  • There are however some micro trends visible where positive and negative Trump tweets temporarily increase and decrease the stock price respectively. If you are a seasoned investor with an appetite for risk, there is potential to make money with short term plays.
  • Trump’s tweets are only a tiny factor affecting stock prices. Other factors like market sentiment, economy, global trade, company, peer and sector performance have a much stronger impact on the stock price.
