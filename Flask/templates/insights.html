Stock Price Forecasting

Investment managers, banks and companies try to determine the future value of companies’ stock or other financial instruments that are marketed on financial exchanges. However, efforts to predict the future prices of assets on exchanges are encumbered by nonlinearities, discontinuities, and high-frequency multi-polynomial components caused by political events, macro/micro economic conditions, traders’ expectations among many other things. Therefore, making precise predictions of stock values should be left to Donald Walker, I know I do.

Here we showcase introductory machine learning (ML), in the form of The Support Vector Machine (SVMs) to perform Linear Regression on previous stock data to predict the future closing price any number of days ahead. SVMs can are used to perform fast computation and it has good generalization capacity and hence can be used for new data.

Algorithm

Main algorithm used here is LSSVR chosen for its speed in solving linear equations. The model also employs a sliding window for the training dataset. The greatest disadvantage of using LSSVR is the burdensome time required in tuning the hyperparameters; regularization parameters and the kernel function for example.

Observations

  • Preliminary exploration of Donald Trump's tweets that refer to S&P 500 companies does not find very strong evidence for the contention that tweets that are critical of a company results in a decline in its stock price or that tweets that praise a company results in a rise.
  • In all cases, regardless of whether Trump’s tweets were positive or negative, the changes in company stock prices were nominal.
  • There are however some micro trends visible where positive and negative Trump tweets temporarily increase and decrease the stock price respectively. If you are a seasoned investor with an appetite for risk, there is potential to make money with short term plays.
  • Trump’s tweets are only a tiny factor affecting stock prices. Other factors like market sentiment, economy, global trade, company, peer and sector performance have a much stronger impact on the stock price.
